Why Ethan?

Experienced Video Editor

Freelancing

My name is Ethan Logue and I started my freelancing career with Roblox when my roommate Will Creighton asked me to make the first trailer for his team's game Kingdom Khaos. Their game would later become a part of the accelerator program where I would be asked to create a second trailer to show off the game in its updated stage.

The recognition that trailer received after being presented publicly had other creators asking Will who the editor was. Once he told me others loved it, I realized I could create a small business out of video editing which has been one of my hobbies for a few years. I joined the scene using Roblox Talent Hub and the Discord server RoDevs where I got multiple offers within two weeks. After building up my name I started getting better offers and have even worked with creators who have over 20 million visits.

No matter how much I was being compensated, my work was always to the best of my ability and I would fulfill all the creators wishes. I use free cam and OBS to record my shots, and I have even done some tinkering in Roblox Studio to get the perfect shot.

Background

In high school, I took two video production classes where we learned the techniques of filming and editing. The editing portion never got too in-depth, but I took it further by learning on my own through tutorials online. We learned a lot about framing and what the different types of framing can subconsciously imply to the viewer.

In my senior year, I was a part of my school's TV Studio which would be shown to the whole school during lunch periods. My role in the studio was editing the weather. This was done with a team of 6 to 8 because we wanted to bring some joy and make other students laugh when they watched the program. We would usually record for the first 15 minutes of class and the last 50 minutes I would quickly edit the weather. Even though I was rushed, I could still turn out a quality product that others enjoyed. I learned a lot of editing techniques and effects during that year which heavily contributed to my success as a freelancer.

Ethan's Projects

This was my first project in the Roblox community that I did. My roommate knew of my ability and thought I would be a good fit to create the trailers for his upcoming game. I edited multiple trailers with several versions each to accommodate newer models and ideas, while simultaneously improving the quality of the trailer each time. I also edited several presentations and smaller clips for the team as needed.

For this position, I was recommended to the owner of Duo Games, Excellence, to edit their gameplay trailer by Polybyte. I went through several versions of the trailer before reaching one we loved. I added every sound effect in manually to better control volume levels, and added a couple different effects throughout to engage the audience.

My close developer friend, Cypraith, started a solo side project to expand his skills and wanted me to create a trailer showing off the diverse stages and front-end effects. This project involved lots of timing with the music we chose, so I put much effort into perfecting it. A fast turnaround time was requested and I got it back to him within two days.

Fun Valley Games reached out to me to create a trailer for their upcoming horror game, "The Lunch Special." I was sent models of the map and tweaked some things to make it more cinematic while recording my footage. This trailer was the lowest budget one, but with what I had I believe it came out really good. They provided me with some of the sound effects they liked and asked me to find other ones completing their vision.

Hoops Life reached out to me shortly after starting my freelancing career and offered me fifty thousand Robux per month for editing TikToks to promote their game and reach new potential players. This was a much different style than the trailers I've been making since it was meant to be humorous and match the community surrounding the game. I took what I've learned from making trailers and my time working on my school's weather channel to create funny edits that still focused on showing off the gameplay mechanics and features.
